International Colloquium at MACE

Share your knowledge. It’s a way to achieve immortality” -Dalai Lama

International Colloquium on Recent Trends in Engineering (ICORTE-2020) is scheduled to be conducted on 13 th and 14 th of March 2020 at Mar Athanasius College of Engineering, Kothamangalam. The Conference is jointly organized by various departments of MACE in association with the Institution of Engineers (India).

ICORTE will be a wonderful platform for people working across various disciplines to express, share and enhance their ideas on recent trends in Engineering. It is an International Colloquium with participants from around the world and would be a unique opportunity to interact with stalwarts in their respective areas.

About MACE

Mar Athanasius College of Engineering is a pioneer institution in central Kerala established in the year 1961. The college is managed by Mar Athanasius College Association and is aided by the Government of Kerala. It is affiliated to APJ Abdul Kalam Kerala Technological University. MACE, as it is popularly known, has grown by leaps and bounds since. It has now six full-fledged departments apart from the auxiliary departments of Mathematics, Humanities and Science.

Places to Visit

Kothamangalam is a wonderful town in the mountains of Kerala, India. Kothamangalam is presently a standout amongst the most essential pioneer focuses of Kerala, with twelve houses of worship, about six old sanctuaries and a couple of sacrosanct mosques.

Bhoothathankettu:

Bhoothathankettu is a dam and a tourist spot in Kerala, India. It is situated outside the village of Pindimana, about 10 km away from the town of Kothamangalam and 50 km away from the main city of Kochi.

Munnar:

Munnar, which was once the summer retreat of the British, is a hill station where three mountain streams conspire to make you hold your breath on the road uphill in God’s Own Country, at an altitude of 5,200 ft above sea level about 70 km away from the town of Kothamangalam.

Thekkady:

Just three hours away from the town of Kothamangalam, Thekkady, located in the Idukki district of Kerala at an altitude of 2800 meters, is a stunning haven for nature lovers and wildlife admirers, lures travelers from across the world.

Idukki Dam:

Located just two hours from Kothamangalam,is built across the Periyar river between two rocky hills Kuravan and Kurathi and is predicted a life of 300 years. It is one of the highest dams in the world and the first arch dam in Asia.
